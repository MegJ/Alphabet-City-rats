How to get an abortion in Texas

What Texas' new abortion law really means for women seaking abortions.

September 29, 2021

Women protest the new anti-abortion bill outside of the Governer's mansion in Austin. Photo credit: Evan L'Roy/The Texas Tribune

by Megan Rochlin

On May 19, 2021, Governor Greg Abbott of Texas signed bill S.B.8, also known as the “heartbeat bill”, into effect. Under the law, any physician who performs an abortion after a fetal heartbeat has been detected is liable to be sued. The law effectively bans abortions after 5-6 weeks of pregnancy, when fetal heartbeats are first detectable. But what does that really mean If you are a woman in Texas?

  • Gestational age is not calculated from the date of conception, it is calculated from the first day of a woman’s last menstrual cycle.

  • Most pregnancy tests aren’t effective until after the first day of a women’s missed period. If a woman takes a pregnancy test on the very first day she misses her period, the gestational age of her fetus will be 3-4 weeks, meaning she now has 1-2 weeks to obtain an abortion.
