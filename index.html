Alphabet City is officially Manhatten's most rat infested neighborhood. It's getting worse.

November 9, 2021

A rat drinks out of a starbucks cup Photo by Mert Guller

by Megan Rochlin

The stretch of sixth street between Avenue C and D is dotted with trees. It is lined on one side with classic brownstones and on the other with tidy apartment buildings. Residents include young professionals, families, and, increasingly, rats.

"I always challenge my fiance to see who can spot the most rats while we walk along the block. I usually get like 14." Caitlin Adder said of the block."

Alphabet City has the title of most rat-infested neighborhood in lower Manhattan. Susan Stetzer, district manager for Community Board 3, acknowledged the problem in an interview with the nypost. She said that the rats “feast on garbage from eateries and scraps left in Tompkins Square and Seward parks.”

Data shows the problem is getting worse, with sightings up 100% in 2021 compared with 2020.

According to Entomologist Benjamin Hottel, the uptick in sightings is due to the lifting of covid restrictions. “As things open up more, you will have more and more food availability,” he said in an interview with Bloomberg.
